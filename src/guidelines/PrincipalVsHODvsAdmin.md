## Real-World Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Student Wins â‚¹1,00,000 in National Hackathon

**HOD Role:**
- Sees the certificate upload
- Approves it for department records
- Tracks it in department analytics
- **Limitation**: Can't see if other departments had similar wins

**Admin Role:**
- Sees the data in database
- Exports report if requested

- **Limitation**: Cannot validate or approve

**Principal Role:**
- Reviews high-prize case

- **Benefit**: Strategic decision on college representation

## Final Answer

### Why Principal Dashboard Exists:

1. **Strategic Leadership**: College needs a leader with full visibility
2. **Resource Allocation**: Fair distribution requires full picture
3. **Quality Standards**: Institutional consistency across departments
4. **External Relations**: Accreditation, rankings, reports need single source
5. **Final Authority**: Someone must have ultimate decision power

### Why It Can't Be HOD:
- Too narrow (one department)
- Can't compare departments
- No institution-wide authority

### Why It Can't Be Admin:
- Technical role, not academic
- No approval authority
- No strategic mandate

### Why All Three Together Work:
- **Principal**: Strategic oversight (big picture)
- **HOD**: Department management (quality control)
- **Admin**: Technical support (system runs)

Each has **distinct responsibilities** that don't overlap but **complement** each other for a complete, efficient system.
